Hey everyone! I built a little macOS app called *RafScreen* that mirrors your iPhone/iPad screen on your Mac via USB cable. It wraps the screen in a realistic device bezel frame — great for demos, presentations, and recordings.

*What it does:*
- Mirrors your iOS device screen in real-time over USB
- Auto-detects your device model and shows the matching bezel (40+ devices supported)
- Screenshot with bezel overlay (Cmd+S) — saves to Desktop
- Screen recording (Cmd+R) — saves .mov to Desktop
- Background themes: Dark, Black, Light, Gray, Sepia, Green Screen
- Hot-swap between devices without restarting

*How to install:*
1. Download the DMG file attached below
2. Open the DMG and drag *RafScreen* to your Applications folder
3. The first time you open it, macOS will block it since it's not from the App Store

*To allow it (one-time setup):*
1. Try opening RafScreen — you'll get a warning that it can't be opened
2. Go to *System Settings > Privacy & Security*
3. Scroll down — you'll see a message saying "RafScreen was blocked from use because it is not from an identified developer"
4. Click *Open Anyway*
5. From now on it will open normally

*To use:*
1. Plug your iPhone/iPad into your Mac via USB
2. Unlock your device and tap *Trust* if prompted
3. RafScreen will detect it automatically and start mirroring

Let me know if you have any issues!
